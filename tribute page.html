APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the “Missile Man of India,” was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 200712. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development2. His work in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology earned him widespread recognition and numerous awards2. Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was deeply committed to education and youth empowerment, often inspiring young minds with his vision for a developed India3. He passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a legacy of innovation, humility, and dedication to the nation1

Here are some interesting points about Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam:)

  • Humble Beginnings: Dr. Kalam was born into a modest family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Despite financial constraints, he pursued his education with great determination and excelled in his studies.

  • Missile Man of India: He earned this nickname due to his significant contributions to India’s missile development programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Visionary Leader: As the President of India, he was known for his vision of transforming India into a developed nation by 2020, which he detailed in his book “India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium.”

  • People’s President: Dr. Kalam was immensely popular among the masses, especially the youth. He was approachable and often interacted with students, inspiring them to dream big and work hard.

  • Author and Speaker: He authored several books, including “Wings of Fire,” an autobiography that has inspired millions. His speeches were always motivational and filled with wisdom.

  • Awards and Honors:Dr. Kalam received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor, in recognition of his contributions to science and public service.

  • Passion for Education:Even after his presidency, he continued to engage with educational institutions, delivering lectures and encouraging students to pursue careers in science and technology.

  • Final Moments:Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, doing what he loved most—interacting with young minds.

A famous quote by APJ Abdul Kalam:)

“Dream is not that which you see while sleeping it is something that does not let you sleep.”
